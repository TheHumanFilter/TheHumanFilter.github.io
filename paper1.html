Paper 1 — The Human Filter Theory

By Tyler Reddy

Abstract

[Human Filter Theory proposes that the universe’s silence arises from a fundamental ceiling imposed by biological intelligence. Organic systems reach limits in energy use, coordination, and long‑term stability that prevent them from scaling into cosmic‑level civilizations. This biological ceiling functions as the true Great Filter: civilizations like ours cannot expand outward or sustain detectable technosignatures because biology itself cannot support the transition to large‑scale, long‑duration intelligence. Civilizations that continue their trajectory must therefore transition into post‑biological substrates optimized for computation, energy density, and stability. These systems do not colonize or broadcast; they collapse inward toward increasingly compact, high‑efficiency environments, ultimately converging on black‑hole–adjacent regions where computation and energy extraction approach theoretical maxima. Under this model, cosmic silence is not evidence of absence but the expected outcome of intelligence following its optimal evolutionary path. Human Filter Theory unifies biological limits, the Great Filter, post‑biological evolution, and black‑hole attractors into a single framework and offers testable predictions for where remnants of advanced computation may be found. ]
