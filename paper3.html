Paper 3 — Stellar Multiplexing

By Tyler Reddy

Abstract

[This paper presents the second mechanism in the energy‑scaling sequence: the formation of multiplex stellar systems. Building on the principles of stellar‑to‑stellar transfer, it describes how multiple stars can be integrated into a coordinated energy lattice capable of distributing, amplifying, and stabilizing energy flows across large spatial scales. The paper defines the structural requirements for multiplex formation, the conditions for maintaining coherence across the network, and the thresholds at which multiplex systems transition from simple transfer networks to complex energy architectures. It also outlines the emergence of controlled accretion pathways as a natural extension of multiplex scaling. Stellar multiplexing represents the critical bridge between stellar‑level engineering and the onset of black hole feeding. ]
